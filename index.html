Lesson 1 of 4
Two kinds of electricity
Before we talk about positive and negative, you need to know that your meter measures two completely separate things at the same time. Think of them as two independent odometers in a car.
The restaurant analogy
Active energy is the food that gets eaten — it does real work, nourishes you, and you pay for it. Reactive energy is the waiter walking back and forth to your table — necessary to deliver the food, takes up effort and space in the restaurant, but you don't eat the waiter. Both happen at the same time. Both get measured.
Active energy
Real power. Does actual work. Turns motors, makes heat, lights bulbs. You pay for this. Measured in kWh.
Reactive energy
Invisible support power. Maintains magnetic fields in motors and coils. Does no useful work. Measured in kVArh.
Key insight
These two tracks are completely independent. A reading on one says nothing about the other. Your meter logs both simultaneously, 24 hours a day.

Active energy: positive vs negative
Active energy has a direction. Power can flow into your house from the grid, or out from your house back to the grid. The meter watches both directions and records them separately.

Lesson 3 of 4
Reactive energy: the invisible current
Reactive energy is not about direction of flow in the same way. It is about the type of load connected to the circuit — specifically whether it has coils of wire inside.
The spring analogy
Imagine pushing a heavy spring. You push it in (energy goes in), then it pushes back (energy returns). You did work, but the spring went nowhere useful. Motors and coils do exactly this — they borrow energy from the grid to build a magnetic field, then return some of it, 50 times per second.
